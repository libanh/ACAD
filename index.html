About Me

Welcome to my website, I am a first year second year Ph.D. student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Washington (UW) where I work in the Emerging Integrated Technologies & Systems Lab with Sajjad Moazeni. developing integrated photonic integrated circuits (IC) for biomedical and communication applications. I was born in Columbus, Ohio, but lived in a few states including Kentucky, Maine, and Arizona before settling in Washington about 11 years ago. Growing up near a Radio Shack as a kid, I remember buying Forrest Mims Circuit Scrapbooks and Mini-Engineering project books and completing them in my free time. After finishing several projects like audio amplifiers, radio receivers, and headphones made of cardboard, aluminum foil, and audio jacks, I felt this was the path for me to pursue and went on to get my degree in ECE. I love playing basketball, learning new things, helping people in my local community learn how to build analog circuits (the coolest kind), and running. At one point in my life (before engineering), I could run a mile in under 5 minutes!

After taking an analog circuit design class my senior year of undergrad, I was inspired by the instructor (RBD- The Analog Goat) and felt that it would be great to share some of the neat circuits from that class among others with other aspiring analog circuit designers. Hopefully youâ€™ll benefit and enjoy some of these circuits as I have :) Analog circuit design can be a challenging field, and being confused is perfectly normal! I have found that strengthening the fundamentals is necessary for developing a strong intuition, and it's my hope that this this website will help in doing so. Remember- it doesn't matter how frustrating the tool being used for simulation can be. It doesn't matter how advanced the circuit may seem. It doesn't matter what others' perception of your understanding is- what matters is your passion, determination and efforts in working to become a better designer day by day.
